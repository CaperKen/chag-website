Advocating for Inclusive Community Living in Nova Scotia

Working to ensure that people with developmental disabilities have the right to live in homes and communities of their choosing.

About Us

The Community Homes Action Group (CHAG) is a coalition of families, advocates, professionals, and community members committed to ending the institutionalization of people with developmental disabilities in Nova Scotia. We work to ensure that every person has access to safe, supported, community‑based living options that respect dignity, autonomy, and human rights.

Our Mission

We promote a future where people with developmental disabilities live full, meaningful lives in their communities. Our mission includes:

  • Ending institutionalization and replacing it with community‑based homes and supports.
  • Ensuring choice in where and with whom people live.
  • Supporting families through information, advocacy, and collaborative planning.
  • Promoting human rights in alignment with the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.

Advocacy and Action

CHAG works with individuals, families, community organizations, and government to advance systemic change. Our efforts include:

  • Policy advocacy to ensure the province fulfills its commitments to community living.
  • Public awareness campaigns highlighting the experiences and rights of people with disabilities.
  • Research and reporting on progress toward closing institutions and expanding community‑based options.
  • Collaboration with the Disability Rights Coalition of Nova Scotia and other partners.

We believe that “there is no justice without disability justice,” and we work to ensure that Nova Scotia moves toward a more inclusive future.
